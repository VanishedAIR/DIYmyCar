How to Change a Cabin Air Filter

Make sure you're wearing gloves and safety glasses to ensure proper protection from harmful chemicals.

How Often Should You Change Your Cabin Air Filter?

  1. If you notice restricted airflow from your climate control vents while driving around, it's probably worth checking the filter. As a rule, they should be replaced every 15,000 to 30,000 miles, or they should be replaced if you notice they are dirty during an inspection. If you want a precise service interval, check your owner's manual or find yours here.

Steps:

  1. Open and, oftentimes, remove the glove box to access the cabin air filter. If unsure, check the owner's manual. This may require a screwdriver, or simply pressing down on a tab or two. (see Fig. 1 for example)
  2. Remove the old cabin filter and any debris. If needed, you may need to vacuum the area.
  3. Install the new filter. Ensure the new filter is placed in the same place as the old filter. Air flow direction is usually indicated by arrows ON the filter (see Fig. 2 for example)
    · If unsure about which filter will fit, you can find the proper cabin filter here.
  4. Finally, install the glove box back in place using the same steps you did for removal. (see Fig. 3 for example)

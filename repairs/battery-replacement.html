How to Replace a Car Battery

Make sure you're wearing gloves and safety glasses to ensure proper protection from harmful chemicals.

Steps:

  1. Before replacing the battery, find the correct replacement battery for your car (Visit this website if you're unsure: Battery Finder)
  2. Identify where the old battery is located. (Usually in the front for most gas-powered cars)
  3. Identify the Positive and Negative Terminals
    · Usually marked with red positive (+) symbol, Negative = (-), see Fig. 1 for example
  4. Remove the negative (-) battery cable from the negative battery terminal using a wrench (for example 10mm)
  5. Now remove the positive (+) battery cable from the positive battery terminal
  6. Before you remove the battery, look around to see if there is something that is securing it in its place, if so remove the retaining device and lift the battery out
    · Caution: A car battery is heavy, and you must avoid tipping or jarring it to prevent spilling any acid it contains. Place the battery upright in a safe location, out of reach of children and pets.
  7. Check the cables and terminals. If you notice damage, you'll need to get them replaced by a professional.
  8. Clean the terminals to remove any buildup or corrosion. First the positive (+) then the negative (-). A small wire brush will come in handy. (see Fig. 2 for example)
  9. Carefully place the new battery in the tray and make sure to align the positive and negative posts with the positive and negative terminals. Once you've oriented the battery correctly, reinstall the retention hardware.
    · The new battery will likely have plastic caps covering the terminals. Remove and place them over the posts of the discharged battery or dispose of them.
  10. Place the positive terminal over the positive post and tighten the fastener with a wrench or ratchet and socket. The connection should be secure so that it doesn't shift, but don't overtighten it.
  11. Place the negative terminal over the negative battery post and tighten it. Check that you've securely connected the terminals to the posts so that they won't shift. Make sure you've secured the battery in the tray to prevent it from moving.
  12. If the battery compartment or the terminal has a cover, put them back in place. Start the car to confirm the battery is working correctly.
  13. If you haven't dropped the used battery off at the parts retailer already, do so now if they offer disposal or recycling services. Otherwise, dispose of or recycle the used battery according to the regulations in your area. Your city or town may also have a recycling center that will accept used car batteries. Fig. 3
    · Click here to find a place near you!
  14. Make sure to replace your car battery every 3-5 years.
